Kennesaw

KENNESAW, GA HAS A HISTORY with racism.

If you grow up in Kennesaw, as 6,400 children and teenagers are according to the 2018 census, you grow up in a city that decided only in 2020 to remove the Confederate Flag from its City Hall.

You grow up in a city whose Main Street has for nearly 50 years been plagued by five years of "heritage" that ended over 150 years ago.

On June 20th, 2020, we will be uniting against racism in Kennesaw.

At 2:30 PM, begin gathering in cars at Swift-Cantrell park. At 3:00 PM we will drive down N Main St, turn onto Watts Dr, head down Cobb Pkwy to Nance Rd, then back to Swift-Cantrell Park.

At 3:30 PM, begin gathering at Swift-Cantrell Park. At 4:00 PM, we'll start walking down Main St. We'll stop at Wildman's Dent Myers to peacefully protest his display of racist symbols, before continuing to Kennesaw City Hall where we'll gather and kneel for 9 minutes. Afterwards, walk back to Swift-Cantrell Park.

Not sure if you should come? Read: 'So glad I did it': Mom takes son, 7, to peaceful protest

If you have any concerns or want to support this protest, please email me at jamespatrickdill@gmail.com.
